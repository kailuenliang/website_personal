About

Kai-Luen Liang is a Chinese-American sound and media artist, educator, and tone scientist based in Los Angeles and Beijing. He received his MFA from California Institute of the Arts in Music Technology, Intelligence, Interaction, and Design as well as a specialization at the Center for Integrated Media.

His work deals with various aspects of computation and memory, surveillance/prediction, algorithmic divination, sound poetry, generative and algorithmic design, and plunder-phonics.

His work has been shown at South By South West(SXSW2017), ARS Electronica(2021), XCOAX Conference for Computation, Communication, Aesthetics & X, Beijing 751 Design Week, Małopolski Ogród Sztuki and the Krakow Academy of Fine Arts Gallery. He has performed all over the world at venues and festivals including the Boiler Room (Beijing 2016), Electric Daisy Carnival(Las Vegas 2013), Shanghai International Jazz Festival, and Clockenflap Music Festival (Hong Kong). His music has been released on SVBKLT, Ran Music, Modern Sky, and Robox Neotech (Berlin).

He currently is associate faculty at California Institute of the Arts, teaching at both the Center For Integrated Media and Music Technology(MTIID).

Blue Marbles(And Other Spherical Objects)


A meditation on modern surveillance, rare earth materials, labor, and shifting landscapes in an age of surveillance capitalism. It looks at the seemingly "mundane" aspects of watching, being watched, and data collection. Blue Marbles repurposes the symbols of modern surveillance branding from Chinese surveillance hardware infomercials/tropes into a reflection on the complex nature of surveillance metadata and hardware.
